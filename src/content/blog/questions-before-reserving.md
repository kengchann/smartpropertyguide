Reserving a pre-selling house-and-lot unit or condo is exciting — but a reservation fee, however small, is still the first real commitment in the buying process. Before you pay it, here are five questions worth getting clear answers to first.

## 1. What exactly does the reservation fee lock in?

Reservation fees typically hold a specific unit or lot at a specific price for a set period (often 30–60 days) while you complete your down payment paperwork. Make sure you understand whether the fee is deductible from your total contract price, and what happens if you decide not to proceed.

## 2. What's the full payment timeline, turnover-to-turnover?

Ask for the complete schedule: down payment period, financing application deadlines, and the estimated turnover date. Pre-selling projects can take a few years to complete — knowing the full timeline up front avoids surprises later.

## 3. What happens if construction is delayed?

Delays happen industry-wide. Ask how the developer communicates updates, and whether there's any provision in the contract for significant delays.

## 4. What financing options are actually available for this specific project?

Not every project supports every financing route — some support Pag-IBIG, some don't; some offer in-house rent-to-own, some don't. Confirm what's actually available for the unit you're looking at, not just what's available "in general."

## 5. What's included, and what's an upgrade?

Model units often show upgraded finishes, fixtures, or built-ins that aren't part of the standard turnover package. Ask specifically what your unit will look like on turnover day versus what's shown in the display unit.

## The bottom line

None of these questions are meant to slow you down — they're meant to make sure the excitement of reserving a home doesn't turn into confusion six months later. A good agent should be happy to walk through all of this with you before you commit.
