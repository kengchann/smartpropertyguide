# Website Guide for Carolyn

This is your simple how-to for updating the Smart Property Guide website yourself — no coding,
no developer needed. Think of it like managing posts on Facebook: you log in, fill in a form,
click a button, and it's live.

---

## 1. How to Log In

1. Go to **`yourwebsite.com/admin`** in your browser (Kenneth will give you the real address
   once the site has its final domain — for now it might look like
   `smart-property-guide.netlify.app/admin`).
2. The first time only: check your email for an invite message from **Netlify**. Click the
   link in that email and set a password. You only need to do this once.
3. After that, every time you visit `yourwebsite.com/admin`, just enter your email and
   password and click **Log In**.
4. You'll land on a dashboard with three sections: **Site Profile & Settings**, **Featured
   Developments**, and **Blog Posts**.

> 🔒 Keep your password private — anyone who logs in can change the live website.

---

## 2. How to Update Your Profile Photo, Cover Photo, and Bio

This is the "make it feel like your Facebook profile" section — one photo behind your name at
the top of the About page (and the homepage background), and one round profile photo, just like
a Facebook cover photo + profile picture.

1. From the dashboard, click **Site Profile & Settings** on the left, then click **"Profile &
   Contact Info"**.
2. To add your **round profile photo**: find the **Profile Photo** box, click **"Choose an
   image"**, and pick a square-ish photo of yourself from your computer.
3. To add your **cover/header photo**: find the **Cover / Header Photo** box the same way. This
   one shows wide, so a landscape photo (a nice property photo, an event photo, anything
   professional) works best.
4. You can also update your **Name, Title, Company, Tagline, Bio, phone, email, WhatsApp/
   Messenger links, PRC license, years of experience, service areas, and specialties** — all on
   this same page.
5. When you're done, click **Publish** → **Publish now** at the top.
6. Give it a couple of minutes (see Section 6), then check the homepage and About page — your
   new photos and info should be live everywhere they appear on the site.

> 💡 Tip: photos that are too small or blurry will look that way on the live site too — use the
> best-quality version of a photo you have.

---

## 3. How to Add a New Featured Development

1. From the dashboard, click **Featured Developments** on the left.
2. Click the green **"+ Development"** button near the top.
3. Fill in the form, box by box:
   - **Project Name** — e.g. "Serenza"
   - **Brand / Community Tag** — e.g. "CrownAsia House & Lot Community"
   - **Development Type** — choose House & Lot or Condominium from the dropdown
   - **Location** — e.g. "Santa Rosa, Laguna"
   - **Starting Price (numbers only)** — just the number, e.g. `4500000` (no ₱ sign, no commas)
   - **Starting Price (shown on site)** — how it should look to visitors, e.g. `From ₱4,500,000`
   - **Bedrooms / Layout** — e.g. "3–4 Bedrooms"
   - **Floor / Lot Area** — e.g. "Lot area: 90–120 sqm"
   - **Payment Terms** — click **"Add"** for each option that applies (Spot Cash, Bank
     Financing, Pag-IBIG Financing, Rent-to-Own)
   - **Status** — Selling / Limited Units Left / Sold Out
   - **Main Photo** — see uploading steps below
   - **Description** — a short paragraph about the development
   - **This is placeholder/sample content** — leave this **unchecked** for real projects (it's
     only used for the demo entries)
4. **To upload the photo:** click the photo box, then **"Choose an image"**. This opens your
   computer's file browser — find the photo file and double-click it. A preview will appear
   once it's uploaded.
5. When everything looks right, click the green **Publish** button in the top-right, then
   **Publish now**.
6. That's it — the new development is live (see timing note in Section 6).

---

## 4. How to Edit or Remove a Development (e.g. Mark as "Sold Out")

**To edit (e.g. mark a project Sold Out):**
1. Click **Featured Developments**, then click on the project you want to change.
2. Change the **Status** dropdown to **Sold Out** (or update any other field).
3. Click **Publish** → **Publish now**.

**To remove a development entirely:**
1. Open the project the same way.
2. Click the red **"Delete entry"** button near the top.
3. Confirm when asked. This cannot be undone, so double-check you have the right one.

> 💡 Tip: If a project is just temporarily sold out but might come back, it's safer to change
> its Status to "Sold Out" rather than deleting it — you can always switch it back later.

---

## 5. How to Write and Publish a Blog Post

1. From the dashboard, click **Blog Posts** on the left.
2. Click **"+ Post"**.
3. Fill in the form:
   - **Title** — the headline of your post
   - **Short Excerpt** — one or two sentences that appear on the blog listing page
   - **Publish Date** — pick today's date, or a future date if you want to schedule it
   - **Author** — usually just "Carolyn Mendoza"
   - **Cover Photo** — same upload steps as above
   - **Save as Draft** — check this box if you're not ready to publish yet (it stays hidden
     from visitors until you uncheck it)
   - **Post Content** — write your article here. You can use the toolbar above the text box to
     make text **bold**, add headings, or make bullet lists
4. Click **Publish** → **Publish now** when ready.

---

## 6. How Long Until Changes Show Up on the Website?

After you click **Publish**, the website automatically rebuilds itself. This usually takes
**1–3 minutes**. If you refresh the live website right after publishing and don't see your
change yet, that's completely normal — just wait a couple of minutes and refresh again.

---

## 7. Something Looks Broken vs. What's Normal

**This is normal, don't worry:**
- The site takes a minute or two to update after you click Publish.
- A photo looking slightly different in size/crop than you expected — you can always re-upload
  a differently-sized version.
- The "Sample" placeholder developments and blog posts you see when you first log in — those
  get replaced with real ones over time.

**Contact Kenneth if:**
- You get an error message when trying to log in or publish something.
- The live website shows a blank page, broken layout, or an error message.
- You published something more than 10 minutes ago and it still isn't showing up.
- Anything else looks or behaves in a way you didn't expect.

When you reach out, it helps to include a screenshot of what you're seeing, and which page or
entry you were working on.
